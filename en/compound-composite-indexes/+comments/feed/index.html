MariaDB Knowledge Base Comments for: Compound (Composite) Indexeshttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/compound-composite-indexes/+comments/feed/en-usTue, 14 Mar 2023 19:47:26 +0000Re: Compound (Composite) Indexeshttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/compound-composite-indexes/+comments/2313<p>Is it worth mentioning that indices have a length constraint?</p> <p>I don't know the maximum off-hand (1024?), but as I recall, compound indices require a space equal to the sum of the maximum length of each column in the index, plus some bytes of overhead.</p> <p>Unless I know it's a query I'm going to do often, I avoid "covering indexes" because complex ones can quickly eat up the max index length, in which case, some of the columns (in longest order?) get quietly truncated.</p> <p>Needless to say, truncated columns in the compound index could easily blow away any advantage there was to a covering index! In a large database (say, the New York City phone book), it would have to do a table scan of all the truncated columns, right?</p> Jan Steinmanhttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/compound-composite-indexes/+comments/2313
