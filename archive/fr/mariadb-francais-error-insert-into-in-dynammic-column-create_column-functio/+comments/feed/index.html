I have used my own method to make SELECT queries, not the UPDATE method.</p> <p>Thridly (here, it's interesting for others). With prepared statement, you can't make :</p> <pre class="fixed">In your query : COLUMN_CREATE(?) MariaDbBlob optionalDataBlob = new MariaDbBlob(&#34;'value', '12'&#34;.getBytes()); sqlQuery.getPreparedStatement().setBlob(4, optionalDataBlob); </pre><p>You have to do many "?" to insert your keys and values. So you have to do :</p> <pre class="fixed">In your query : COLUMN_CREATE(?, ?) //or more &#34;?&#34; //And you just insert strings. sqlQuery.getPreparedStatement().setString(4, &#34;value&#34;); sqlQuery.getPreparedStatement().setString(5, &#34;12&#34;); </pre><p>That's all.
