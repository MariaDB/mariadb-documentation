It has some quirks and has gotten slower over the years, so I don't use it very much now.</p> <p>My favourite is Sequel Pro (<a href="http://www.sequelpro.com">http://www.sequelpro.com</a>). It's free. I wish it could identify foreign keys with a tooltip, like phpMyAdmin does, but it at least gives you a link, so you're one click away.</p> <p>Another one I like is Querious. (<a href="http://www.araelium.com/querious/">http://www.araelium.com/querious/</a>) I would use it more often but for a silly reason: i can't remember its name! I think it costs something, but not very much. It has a nice boxes-and-lines graphical schema view that Sequel Pro lacks.</p> <p>I also have downloaded other tools from the Apple Store, such as Toad, Metabase, LamaFree, and more. Most of them, I fire up, play with for a bit, then go back to my favourites above.</p> <p>Hope this is helpful!</p> Jan Steinmanhttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/mac-database-administration/+comments/2503
