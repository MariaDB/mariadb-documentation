MariaDB Knowledge Base Comments for: SHARE PERFORMANCE ACROSS 2 SERVERShttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/share-performance-across-2-servers/+comments/feed/en-usTue, 13 Feb 2024 01:11:34 +0000Re: SHARE PERFORMANCE ACROSS 2 SERVERShttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/share-performance-across-2-servers/+comments/29<p>The only ways to share data between two servers are: - Using MySQL cluster - Using the federated storage engine</p> <p>However, in both cases things will get slower when you do joins across data that is in two machines.</p> <p>The ways to solve your problem depends on what storage engine you are using, the size of the buffers and how big your table_open_cache and table_definition_cache is.</p> <p>With some storage engines, like MyISAM, you can put different tables in different key caches which ensures that using one big table doesn't flush out the others.</p> Michael Wideniushttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/share-performance-across-2-servers/+comments/29
