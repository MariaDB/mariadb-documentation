MariaDB Knowledge Base Comments for: Setting up a Replica with Mariabackuphttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/setting-up-a-replica-with-mariabackup/+comments/feed/en-usMon, 19 Aug 2024 03:44:38 +0000Re: Setting up a Replication Slave with Mariabackuphttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/setting-up-a-replica-with-mariabackup/+comments/5331<p>For version 10.2, 10.3 and 10.4, it seems that mariabackup is introducing random corruption in the tablespace. This should be mentioned as a warning or this article removed until it is fixed as the corruption will cause latent crashes, which is even worse than the backup failing altogether. Running mysqlcheck --auto-repair on the backup isn't enough to detect corruption, only mysqloptimize could find it. Running <a href="/kb/en/mariadb-10411-release-notes/">mariadb 10.4.11</a> at the moment and the bugs I hit are documented on the bug tracker already (<a href="https://jira.mariadb.org/browse/MDEV-18193">MDEV-18193</a>, <a href="https://jira.mariadb.org/browse/MDEV-19978">MDEV-19978</a>, <a href="https://jira.mariadb.org/browse/MDEV-23653">MDEV-23653</a>).</p> Jerome Poulinhttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/setting-up-a-replica-with-mariabackup/+comments/5331
