MariaDB Knowledge Base Comments for: Unknown database 'mysql'https://mariadb.com/kb/en/unknown-database-mysql/+comments/feed/en-usMon, 02 Sep 2024 12:36:02 +0000Re: Unknown database 'mysql'https://mariadb.com/kb/en/unknown-database-mysql/+comments/5834<p>I assume you are following <a href="https://wiki.archlinux.org/title/MariaDB">https://wiki.archlinux.org/title/MariaDB</a>.</p> <p>I can't tell why mysql is "Unknown database".</p> <p>There's some really horrible recommendations on the wiki in archlinux.</p> <p>If its a new install, I recommend removing your datadir contents and reinstall with <code class="fixed" style="white-space:pre-wrap">mariadb-install-db --user mysql --skip-test-db</code>, then start MariaDB.</p> <p>Don't use mysql_secure_installation (skip-test-db covers most of it), and its a less than maintained script.</p> <p>Create a normal user for yourself with <code class="fixed" style="white-space:pre-wrap">sudo -u mysql mariadb</code> and the <a href="/kb/en/create-user/">CREATE USER</a> with appropriate <a href="/kb/en/grant/">GRANTS</a>.</p> Daniel Blackhttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/unknown-database-mysql/+comments/5834
