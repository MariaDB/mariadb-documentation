MariaDB Knowledge Base Comments for: Indexing on low cardinality columns is degrading the performancehttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/indexing-on-low-cardinality-columns-is-degrading-the-performance/+comments/feed/en-usMon, 12 May 2025 13:55:09 +0000Re: Indexing on low cardinality columns is degrading the performancehttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/indexing-on-low-cardinality-columns-is-degrading-the-performance/+comments/6729<p>First note that the 10.7 release series is at the end of life.</p> <p>The 11.0 release series and newer, like the 11.4 LTS release series of MariaDB have improved optimizer functions capable of handling the OR criteria of your JOIN in a much better way estimating the cost better and likely to generate a better query plan.</p> <p>Yes low cardinality should usually be shunned, except in the case where the dataset of the table can be retreived from just the index.</p> <p>Using <a href="/kb/en/analyze-format-json/">ANALYZE FORMAT=JSON {query}</a> can help see where either r_ amount, the real, differers from the estimate without the r_ prefix.</p> <p><a href="/kb/en/optimizer-trace-guide/">Optimizer trace</a> allows a trace of how the query plan was made.</p> <p>Strongly recommend updating to the 11.4 version in a test environment and measuring how well or otherwise the query performs there. Include ANALYZE or optimizer trace if still getting suboptimal queries.</p> Daniel Blackhttps://mariadb.com/kb/en/indexing-on-low-cardinality-columns-is-degrading-the-performance/+comments/6729
